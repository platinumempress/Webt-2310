Coral Bioluminescence

Bioluminescence in coral is when corals emit light through chemical reactions. This glow can attract prey, deter predators, or aid symbiotic relationships. These light displays enhance reef beauty and play a crucial role in their ecological dynamics.

Around the World

Coral reefs are found in tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide, from Australia's Great Barrier Reef to the Maldives' atolls. These vibrant underwater cities host diverse marine life, providing shelter, food, and breeding grounds for countless species.

Reefs as a Habitat

Often called the "rainforests of the sea," coral reefs boast rich biodiversity. They provide essential habitats for many marine organisms, offering protection, breeding sites, and abundant food resources, crucial for the survival of numerous species.
