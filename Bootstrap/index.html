Coral Bioluminescence

Bioluminescence in coral is when corals emit light through chemical reactions. This glow can attract prey, deter predators, or aid symbiotic relationships. These light displays enhance reef beauty and play a crucial role in their ecological dynamics.

Around the World

Coral reefs are found in tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide, from Australia's Great Barrier Reef to the Maldives' atolls. These vibrant underwater cities host diverse marine life, providing shelter, food, and breeding grounds for countless species.

Reefs as a Habitat

Often called the "rainforests of the sea," coral reefs boast rich biodiversity. They provide essential habitats for many marine organisms, offering protection, breeding sites, and abundant food resources, crucial for the survival of numerous species.

Coral Reefs' Function in the World

Coral reefs are vital for our planet's health. They protect coastlines from erosion, support local economies through tourism and fishing, contribute to global biodiversity, and are sources of new medicines. Their ecological services are invaluable, highlighting the need for conservation.

Dangers to the Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are under severe threat from climate change, pollution, overfishing, and destructive practices. Rising sea temperatures and ocean acidification cause widespread coral bleaching, while plastic pollution and chemical runoff cause further harm. The loss of coral reefs would devastate marine life and human communities. To help protect these vital ecosystems, please consider donating to coral reef conservation organizations.
